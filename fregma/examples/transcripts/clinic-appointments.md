Facilitator: Let's map out how appointment scheduling works at the clinic.

Office Manager: A patient books an appointment with a doctor. We identify patients by their medical record number, which is assigned at registration.

Facilitator: And doctors?

Office Manager: Each doctor has a provider ID issued by the state medical board. Every doctor has a specialty -- things like cardiology, dermatology, family medicine, orthopedics, or pediatrics. A doctor has exactly one specialty.

Facilitator: How are appointments identified?

Office Manager: Each appointment gets a unique confirmation number when it's created.

Facilitator: What information does an appointment carry?

Office Manager: The date, a time slot, and a reason for the visit. The reason is free text -- whatever the patient describes when they call. Every appointment must have a date and a time slot. The time slot is one of: 8:00, 8:30, 9:00, 9:30, 10:00, 10:30, 11:00, 11:30, 13:00, 13:30, 14:00, 14:30, 15:00, 15:30, 16:00, or 16:30.

Facilitator: Can a patient have multiple appointments?

Office Manager: Yes, a patient can have many appointments over time. But a patient can only have one appointment per time slot on a given day -- no double-booking from the patient side.

Facilitator: What about the doctor side?

Office Manager: Same rule. A doctor can only see one patient per time slot on a given day.

Facilitator: Are there appointment statuses?

Office Manager: Yes. An appointment has a status: scheduled, checked-in, in-progress, completed, cancelled, or no-show. Every appointment starts as scheduled. A completed appointment may also have a follow-up note, which is free text.

Facilitator: Do you track which room the appointment is in?

Office Manager: We do. Each exam room has a room number. A doctor is assigned to a room for the day, but rooms can be shared across doctors on different days.

Facilitator: One last question -- do patients always see the same doctor?

Office Manager: Not necessarily. A patient might see Dr. Patel one visit and Dr. Chen the next. But we do track a "primary doctor" for each patient. Every patient must have a primary doctor on file.
